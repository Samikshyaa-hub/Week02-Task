What is Mo:Mo

Momo is a type of dumpling, originally from Tibet, that is extremely popular in Nepal and other Himalayan regions. It consists of a dough wrapper filled with meat (like chicken, buffalo, or pork) or vegetables (like cabbage, onions, or potatoes).

Why Nepali loves Mo:Mo?

Nepalis love momo for its delicious taste, affordability, versatility, and social significance. It's a popular dish available in various forms and flavors, making it a staple in Nepali cuisine and a favorite for many. Here's a more detailed look: Taste and Variety: Momos are known for their delicious taste, with a wide range of fillings like chicken, buff, vegetables, and even chocolate. They can be steamed, fried, or served in spicy sauces like chili momo or jhol momo. This variety caters to different preferences and makes it a versatile dish. Affordability: Momos are generally inexpensive, making them accessible to a wide range of people. A plate of momos can be found for a relatively low price, making it a popular choice for a quick and satisfying meal, according to Altitude Himalaya. Social Aspect: Momo is often enjoyed in social settings, with families and friends gathering to make and share them. It's a dish that brings people together and is a common sight at gatherings and festivals.

How to make Mo:Mo

    1.Make Dough - Mix flour, salt, and water. Knead into soft dough and rest it.
    2.Make Filling - Mix minced meat or veggies with onion, garlic, ginger, oil, and salt.
    3.Roll Dough - Make small balls and roll into thin circles.
    4.Fill & Fold - Put filling in the center and fold into dumpling shapes.
    5.Steam - Steam momos for 10-12 minutes till cooked.
    6.Serve - Enjoy hot with spicy chutney!
